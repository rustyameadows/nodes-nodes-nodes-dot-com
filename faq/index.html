General

What the product is

What is Nodes Nodes Nodes?

A local-first Mac app for building, running, debugging, and reviewing AI workflows on an infinite canvas.

Who is it for?

Creative AI power users who want more structure than scattered provider-specific interfaces and output folders.

What makes it different?

It combines infinite-canvas workflows, queue and debug visibility, and local asset review in one Mac app.

What can I do in the app?

Work with text, data, images, video, files, local tools, provider runs, and compare-oriented output review.

Platform and availability

Where it runs and what stage it is in

Is it available right now?

Yes. It is a very early macOS alpha, and the GitHub repo is the current source of truth for access.

Does it run in the browser?

No. The current product is a desktop app.

Does it support Windows or Linux?

Not in the current public site scope. The outward-facing messaging is macOS first.

Can I download the alpha today?

Yes, with caveats. The current very early macOS alpha path runs through the GitHub repo, not a polished consumer download flow.

Workflow and providers

What the current product covers

What providers does it support?

At a high level: OpenAI, Google Gemini, and Topaz.

How do provider keys work?

You bring your own provider credentials. The packaged app is designed around app settings, and source-run development can also use environment variables.

Does it support local models?

The public site keeps that claim conservative. The current outward-facing promise is local-first workflows, local files, and local tools.

Does it support collaboration or cloud sync?

Not in the current version. The product is local-first and single-user today.

Privacy and local-first

How the site talks about data and scope

Why describe it as local-first?

The product is designed around local projects, local workflow state, local files, and a desktop runtime.

Is this site collecting waitlist signups yet?

No. The waitlist CTA is still a placeholder while the download path points to the GitHub repo.

Where should I look for policy details?

The privacy page is present as a draft placeholder until formal policy language is adopted.
